Yotsuba&! - Azuma Kiyohiko

best peace of fiction ever. Seriously and in all honesty, this is genuinely my utmost favorite manga ever. its a great read on any mood. sad, happy, bored, and whatnot. Yotsuba is a great character, the humor is on point, the dialogue feels real, and the overall manga just makes me feel like im a kid again. Yotsuba's interactions with all the other side characters are also really funny and her plethora of adventures is really fun to read. i tend to forget about this series though since it doesnt get updated often but its one of the very few mangas where im genuinely excited to read when i see that theres a new chapter out.

The Dangers in My Heart - Sakurai Norio

the only actually good rom-com. i'm a big fan of rom-coms but most of them feel like the author hasnt been in touch or in contact with a woman for their entire life. this reads like the characters are genuinely teenagers and not just exagerrated to be really dumb. the cringe stuff that happens and they do doesnt detract from the experience because they arent done horribly and actually help in developing the plot and relationship as a whole. it's a bit of a slow burn but the progression is really satisfying and the main characters are really cute together :)

The Fable - Minami Katsuhisa

my favorate Seinen manga. i'm not a big fan of a lot of Seinen since most are really grim or gory for no rhyme or reason which i really dislike. this one though isnt like that at all and isnt usually too over-the-top in its presentation. what it is though is an equally dark and funny series with a really mature outlook on its setting. it doesnt take itself too seriously but it knows the appropriate times when it needs to which makes this a great read. the only other Seinen that i'd consider replacing this with is Ajin which is also a pretty great manga to read. i dont know the status of this, whether it still ongoing and whatnot, but i hope it isnt done yet because id love to see more from this story.

Dandadan - Tatsu Yukinobu

out of all the series thats in the 'Shounen Jump' lineup right now, i think this has the most potential to reach the heights of other series like one punch man season 1 or the peak of jojo's bizzare adventure back in like 2016 or 2017 or something. this has all the ingredients to garner a huge fanbase and for good reasons. an actually entertaining and great romance plot which is rare on 'Shounen Jump', solid plot, great cast, and amazing art. based from the ongoing and already foreshadowed plot points, it seems like this'll be one of those long running 400 chapter esc series and i hope it is. 'Shounen Jump' currently is lacking another massive series right now other than 'One Piece' and this seems like a great contender to be running alongside it.

if realism is the only bar for sports mangas then i wouldve gone for 'Ao aoshi' or 'Haikyuu' instead of this one but thank god it isnt because this series is genuinely hype as hell. it is absolutely edgy as hell and bordering most of the times between being somewhat feasible to physically impossible moves but regardless, the characters and their rivalries along with the amazing art makes this a better read for me than the other aforementioned series.

Kagurabachi - Hokazono Takeru

if 'Jujutsu Kaisen' was still ongoing and if the ending for it was a bit better then i wouldve gone with that but since those didnt happen im going with this one. the notoriety of this series starting with a meme clowning on it was, looking back at it, a great event because it made people pay attention to this and thankfully it was able to deliver regardless. the art is phenomenal, the plot is great, and the power system is cool. i see this running around the same ballpark as 'Jujutsu Kaisen', ending at around 250 or so chapters but still, wherever this story takes me, i am very much along for the ride.

manhwa:

my thoughts on these series are gonna be a lot shorter, and there won’t be extra images since the panels are long-strip. i don’t read as much manhwa as i do manga, but i still really like them, so i’m listing them anyway.

Wind Breaker - Jo, Yongseok

this is like the same case as 'Blue Lock' where i have no idea how true or possible whatever they say or do on here but it still slaps so i dont care. the art is great here and the presentation is on point. it's funny to me how dramatic and over-the-top a series about biking can be (they have gangs and whole international corporations as antagonists lmao) but still the delivery is fire so its fine to me.

Lookism - Park, Tae-Jun

i should honestly list this as two seperate series because of how distinctly different the first and second half are to each other. the first half of this is a slice-of-life comedy with some drama and romance about being bullied, ugly, and the concept of "lookism" in society. the second half is about teenage gangwars, inhuman superpowers, and a lot of fights. i still very much like what it is now since its a good turn-off-your-brain read but ngl i still miss the early lookism days.

The Greatest Estate Developer - Moon, Back-kyung & Kim, Hyunsoo

funniest manhwa hands down. its your typical slap stick over the top comedy but the delivery of this isnt cringy so that sets itself apart. it knows when to take itself seriously and slow down the pacing for the emotional parts and then ramp it up again for the comedy and even action.

Villains Are Destined to Die - Gwon, Gyeoeul & SUOL

only 'Otome' (japanese word thats also used for this genre, basically means content targeted for women) i actively follow. i've read a few others but none has been able to capture the magic this one has. the art is phenomenal but the main thing about it is the story, the drama and the romance in this is top notch and i like all the female main character's love interests. it also has its light side sometimes, taking its time to slow down and chill and whatnot but the drama is still where its strength lies.
